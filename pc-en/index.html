Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy (the “Policy”) explains how the website and/or program (the “Program”) handles information related to your use. By accessing the Program, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy.

1. Scope and purpose

The Program is publicly accessible on the Internet, but it is designed primarily for internal use by employees or sales representatives of the organization it is intended for. This Policy aims to clearly explain what information is (or is not) collected and how confidentiality is protected.

2. No intentional collection of personal information

The Program does not intentionally collect or store personal information. It does not require account creation, does not provide user forms intended to collect user information, and does not store usage history.

• No user accounts are created.

• No user database is maintained.

• No profiles, preferences, or usage history are recorded by the Program.

3. Automatically processed technical information

Like any website, certain technical information may be processed automatically by hosting infrastructure, intermediary services (e.g., a CDN), and/or your browser in order to deliver content and ensure basic site functionality.

Depending on the technical environment, this information may include:

• IP address (often within standard technical logs);

• device type, browser, and operating system;

• date and time of requests;

• diagnostic information (errors, performance, loading);

• pages or resources accessed (at the network-request level).

The Program is not intended to identify users from this information and does not use it to build individual profiles.

4. Cookies and similar technologies

The Program does not intentionally use cookies for advertising tracking or profiling. However, technical cookies may be set by third-party services or external sites when you click links (e.g., authentication, security, site-specific preferences).

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Blocking certain cookies may affect how external sites linked from the Program function.

5. Links to third-party websites and tools

The Program contains links to third-party websites, applications, or tools (including official internal resources that may require authentication). When you click a link, you leave the Program and are subject to the third party’s privacy policies and terms of use.

The Program’s operator does not control these sites and is not responsible for their privacy practices, content, or security.

6. Security

The Program’s operator applies reasonable measures to keep the Program functional and reduce security risks (e.g., development best practices, limiting input features, standard hosting).

However, no system is completely free of risk. You are responsible for using a secure device, avoiding sharing sensitive information through the Program, and complying with any applicable security rules in your work environment.

7. Confidentiality and sensitive information (user obligations)

You are responsible for respecting the confidentiality of information you access as part of your work. In particular, you agree not to:

• enter, copy, transmit, or publish personal, confidential, or sensitive information through the Program;

• share internal information (e.g., procedures, pricing, offers, documents, access) with unauthorized persons;

• use the Program in a way that could lead to a data breach or privacy violation.

8. Children and minors

The Program is not intended for minors and is not designed to collect information about children. If you believe a minor has provided personal information through a channel related to the Program, please notify the Program’s operator so appropriate measures can be assessed.

9. Data retention

Because the Program does not intentionally collect or store personal information, there is no specific user-data retention policy at the Program level.

Technical logs may exist at the hosting or third-party service level used to deliver the site. Their retention period is determined by those providers and their operational practices.

10. Your rights

Depending on applicable laws (including in Québec and Canada), you may have rights regarding your personal information (access, correction, withdrawal of consent, etc.).

Since the Program does not maintain user accounts or a personal database, the Program’s operator may be unable to identify or retrieve personal information about you, except to the extent that certain technical information may be available through hosting (if applicable).

11. Changes to this Policy

This Policy may be updated at any time. Any changes become effective once posted on this page. You are responsible for reviewing this page periodically.

12. Contact

If you have any questions about this Policy, you may contact the Program’s operator using the internal contact details provided.
